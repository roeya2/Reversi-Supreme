How to Play Reversi

Objective: Have the most pieces of your color on the board when the game ends.

Rules:

  • Black always moves first.
  • Place your piece on an empty square so that it forms a line (horizontal, vertical, or diagonal) with another piece of your color, sandwiching one or more of your opponent's pieces in between.
  • All the sandwiched opponent's pieces are flipped to your color.
  • A move is only valid if it flips at least one opponent piece.
  • If you cannot make a valid move, your turn is passed to the opponent.
  • The game ends when neither player can make a valid move (usually when the board is full or only one color remains).
  • The player with the most pieces of their color wins.

Tips:

  • Corners are the most valuable squares; they can never be flipped back.
  • Edges are also strong positions.
  • Try to limit your opponent's valid moves (mobility).
  • Sometimes having fewer pieces but better positions is advantageous.
  • Avoid placing pieces in squares directly adjacent to empty corners (like C2, B1, G2, B7, etc.) unless necessary, as this often allows your opponent to take the corner later.
